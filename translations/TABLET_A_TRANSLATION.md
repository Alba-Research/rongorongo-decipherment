This line connects the solar realm to the spirit world.

## Section 3: INTERPRETATION

### Genre: Epic *Patautau*

Tablet A belongs to the *patautau* genre -- the ritual recitation chant that was the primary mode of rongorongo performance. The *patautau* was recited at ceremonies by a *tangata rongorongo* (literate specialist) while holding the tablet. Key genre markers:

1. **Refrain structure**: The *rararau... eena... ara* sequence in Aa7 repeats four times with variation, a hallmark of chant performance where the audience responds between strophes.
2. **Exclamatory particles**: *ie* ("yes!") appears 10+ times, *ei* ("hey!") 2 times -- these are performer's interjections that punctuate the recitation.
3. **Cosmological vocabulary density**: sun, sky, world, shadow, life, flame, path -- the full Polynesian cosmological register is deployed.
4. **Direct address**: the 2SG pronoun *oe* ("you") appears 8 times, creating a dialogical structure between reciter and addressee (the deceased? the god? the audience?).
5. **Length**: at 2,364 glyphs, this is the longest tablet -- consistent with a major ceremonial text, not a fragment or administrative record.

The classification as "epic" (rather than simple patautau) is justified by the narrative arc, the funerary subject matter, and the sustained cosmic imagery across 16 lines.

### Narrative Structure

The tablet divides into four movements:

**I. Invocation and Offering (Aa1--Aa3)**: The chant opens with offerings (food, bamboo, fruit) and cosmological declarations (shine, true breath of world). The cave (*ana*) and cradle (*ute*) establish the birth-and-death poles. The triple *rarara* and triple *oa* are performative affirmations.

**II. The Cosmic Vision (Aa4--Aa6)**: The central recto section describes a vision of the world (*ao* x4), blooming and rising, with the sun and shadow as dual principles. The first-person *au* enters: the reciter witnesses the cosmic spectacle. The key phrase *i raa ata* ("in sun, shadow") encodes the core dualism.

**III. The Path Refrain -- Seizure of the Spirit (Aa7--Aa8)**: The climactic refrain. The compound *rararau* (seize-chant) alternates with *eena... ara* ("those... path") four times, building to the arrival of the lineage (*uri*). The verbs *rarau* (seize), *rate* (miss), *oo* (pierce) describe the violent moment of spirit-capture. The earth oven (*maoa*) signals the funerary feast. The recto ends with the command *ea mai* ("rise, come!") -- the ancestors are summoned.

**IV. Sky, Death, and Return (Ab1--Ab8)**: The verso reverses the direction. The sky (*rai* x6) replaces the earth. The feast for the dead (*uru*) is named. The descent into Ab3 (die, sink, smell, rumble) reaches the nadir. Ab4 names the *tahua* (ceremonial place) -- the tablet identifies its own ritual context. Ab6's onomatopoeic *mu... emu... mu* sequence enacts the sinking into silence. Ab7 reaches the *ahu* (altar) and commands *uea* ("lift!"). Ab8 closes with the counting-stick (*rao*), the temporal adverb *oira* ("before"), and fading demonstratives -- the chant dissolves into the ancestral past.

### Cosmological Landscape

The text maps a three-tiered Polynesian cosmos:

| Realm | Vocabulary | Lines |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Te Rai** (Sky) | rai, raa, rara, ura, ea | Ab1-Ab2, Aa2, Aa6 |
| **Te Ao** (World/Daylight) | ao, ara, ahua, hua, rau, tahua | Aa3-Aa5, Ab4-Ab5 |
| **Te Po** (Night/Underworld) | po, mu, emu, emae, eo, uri, rano | Ab3, Ab6, Aa8 |

The movement is not linear but cyclical: the text rises (Aa2 sun), descends (Ab3 death), rises again (Ab7 altar/lift), and fades (Ab8 before-time). This mirrors the Polynesian conception of time as spiral rather than linear -- each cycle of death and rebirth passes through the same cosmic stations.

### Ethnographic Parallels

1. **Maori *karakia mate*** (death incantations): The structure of Tablet A closely resembles the Maori funerary karakia, which guide the spirit (*wairua*) from the body to Te Reinga (the underworld entrance). The Maori karakia similarly employ: cosmological landmarks (sun, path, cave), direct address to the deceased, refrains with variation, and a final invocation commanding the spirit to rise.

2. **Mangareva *rongorongo*** (chanted genealogies): The Mangarevan tradition, geographically closest to Rapa Nui, preserves chants called *rongorongo* that combine genealogy (*uri*) with cosmological narrative. Tablet A's interweaving of lineage vocabulary with cosmic imagery is consistent with this tradition.

3. ***Umu ta'u*** (year-oven ceremony): The *maoa* (opening of the earth oven) in Aa8 may reference the annual *umu ta'u* ceremony, where food was cooked in earth ovens to feed both the living and the ancestral spirits. This was the principal calendrical festival of pre-contact Rapa Nui.

4. **Barthel's (1960) patautau reconstructions**: Barthel recorded fragmentary oral patautau from the last Rapa Nui elders who remembered the chant tradition. These fragments share vocabulary with Tablet A: *raa* (sun), *ara* (path), *ata* (shadow), *ahu* (platform), and the exclamatory *ie*.

5. **Thomson's (1891) testimony**: Ure Vaeiko, the last alleged rongorongo reader, recited from Tablet A for Paymaster Thomson aboard the USS Mohican in 1886. While the recitation was likely improvised rather than a true reading, Thomson recorded that the text concerned "ceremonies and the gods" -- consistent with our epic patautau classification.
