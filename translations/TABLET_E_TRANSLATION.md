# TABLET E -- KEITI (The Notched One)
**Provenance:** Collected on Rapa Nui (Easter Island), named "Keiti" (notched/incised) in the indigenous tradition. Currently held at the Museo Nacional de Historia Natural, Santiago, Chile. One of the best-preserved tablets, with a distinctive notch on one edge.
**Corpus:** 1,091 glyphs, 17 lines (9 recto + 8 verso), 95.1% grid coverage
**ALBA Grille v3:** 48 signs, Viterbi 5-layer bidirectional segmenter

**Genre:** ATE (funerary chant) -- the only tablet classified in this genre
**Leave-one-tablet-out:** Z=-0.08, p=0.526 (anomalous -- see Interpretation)

## Section 1: CONNECTED READING

### Recto (Er1--Er9)

**Er1** (Opening invocation -- refrain established)
> There, there -- there -- there, there, narrate -- [...] there, narrate -- "utu" "tututu" shine, narrate.

*The opening line establishes the structural skeleton of the recto: the "tara t" refrain appears after each image cluster. Decodable fragments include ra (there/sun) as the dominant deictic, and the tara (narrate) verb that defines the genre. The sequence "utu" may encode utu (retribution/payment -- a funerary offering concept) and the reduplicated "tututu" (stand-stand-stand) an emphatic presence marker. Multiple unknown glyphs (040, 630, 300-series) frame the decodable content, suggesting logographic genre markers flanking phonetic text.*

**Er2** (Sugarcane, sea, and light -- formulaic enumeration)
> Sugarcane -- in sew/string -- there, there, narrate -- sea, stand [...] there, narrate -- leaf/hundred -- strike -- let! -- shine, narrate.

*Three phrases, each ending with the ra-[430]-t-[380] clausula. The word "to" (sugarcane -- a funerary offering), "tui" (to sew/string -- threading offerings?), "tai" (sea), "rau" (leaf/hundred), and "ka" (let!/imperative) emerge between unknown structural markers (203-series). The pattern suggests a formulaic enumeration of offerings or ritual acts, each punctuated by the narrative refrain.*

**Er3** (Continuation of formulaic pattern)
> [...] hand/take [...] shine shine [...] narrate [...] strike [...] narrate [...]

*Three phrases again marked by code 203 and clausulae. The word "ri" (hand/take) appears. The doubled ra-ra could read "raaraa" (brilliant) -- solar vocabulary that appears even in funerary contexts as metaphor for death and rebirth. Code 071-066-071 remains unresolved but appears specifically on E, possibly a funerary logogram.*

**Er4** (Narrative section -- departure from formula)
> [...] narrate [...] it.is [...] of [...] fruit, sun, this [...] of form fruit [...] fruit [...] path [...] in sun

*This line breaks from the formulaic pattern into narrative content. Key words: "ko" (it is), "hua" (fruit -- ritual offering or metaphor for soul), "ra na" (sun + this), "a hua" (of fruit / a form). The sequence "e ou eu" (go, move) suggests verbs of departure consistent with funerary language -- the departed soul's journey beginning. The line reads as a transitional passage: from enumeration of offerings to narration of the soul's departure.*

**Er5** (Vision of the afterworld)
> Eat, there, yes! Red flame, awakening of the path. True -- "iira" -- true sky [...] you -- eat, bay/outrigger? That one -- [...]

*The opening shows a series of 204/205-marked phrases each followed by "ra" (sun/there): a litany of invocations to the sun -- calling it to guide the departed. After double separators (700-700), the tone shifts: "aai" (eat -- ritual consumption?), "ura" (red flame), "aara" (awakening), "ara" (path), "oa" (true), "rai" (sky), "oe" (you). The vocabulary paints a vivid afterworld scene: the true sky, awakening on the path of red flame. The word "ama" (bay/outrigger) may reference the spirit's vessel.*

**Er6** (The solar sentence -- bidirectionally confirmed, zero uncertainty)
> Sun, in this, in come/from, in the path -- [...] shine, narrate -- yes!

> **Confirmed reading:**
> Rapa Nui: **raa i na i-mai i ara... rara tara... aa**
> Translation: "The sun, in this time, comes along the path... it shines, the narrator speaks... yes!"

*This is the most important line on the tablet and one of the most complete sentences in the entire rongorongo corpus. The bidirectional Viterbi algorithm converges from both forward and backward passes with zero uncertainty markers. The sentence is grammatically well-formed Rapa Nui (N PREP DEM PREP-DIR PREP N... V V... EXCL). The verb "tara" (narrate) confirms the funerary genre: the sun itself narrates the story of the departed. The word "mai" (come/from) indicates motion toward the speaker -- the funeral is a welcoming, not a farewell. The exclamation "aa" (yes!) closes the sentence -- the mourners' affirmation. The second half of the line contains a systematic enumeration ([450]-consonant sequences), possibly naming ancestors or stations on the soul's journey.*

**Er7** (Rhythmic triple repetition)
> in [...] strike strike and/with, strike strike [...] and-of [...] and-of [...] and-of [...] [verb] [...] [verb] [...] [verb] it.is it.is [...] sun [...] there, narrate [...] of this particle

*A striking triple repetition dominates the opening: [092]-ma-a-[670] three times, then e-[670] three times. This AAA-BBB structure is characteristic of chanted performance. The decoded "ma a" (and/with + of) or "maa" (food/sustenance) repeats with each pair. The doubled "ko ko" (it is, it is) is an emphatic assertion. The sequence "a na u" near the end may encode "a nau" (a form of anau = chant/recite).*

**Er8** (The cave and the flame -- structural climax)
> Cave, in [...] there -- barren/sterile? Leaf/hundred? Sun, yes! Awakening -- there, true, pierce of red flame -- the rain? There -- in, and with --

*This dense line contains four clausulae (ra-[430]-t-[380]) -- the highest concentration on any line. Each short phrase ends with the funerary refrain, suggesting accelerating rhythm. The juxtaposition of ana (cave), ura (red/flame), raa (sun), aara (awakening), and ua (rain) composes a cosmological passage: the sun awakens in the cave (underworld), pierces through with red flame, and rain falls. This is consistent with Polynesian afterlife imagery where the spirit descends into the cave (Po) and rises again.*

**Er9** (Closing of recto)
> [...] hand/take [...] sun, in [...] [...] particle [...] and-of sun, sun [...] in sun

*The final recto line begins with structural markers (380-739 pairs -- the funerary refrain marker combined with the unknown 739). Decodable words: "i u" (in/at + particle), "ma o ra" (and/with of sun), "ai" (who/by). The line closes with "i ra" (in the sun / towards the sun) -- the departed soul is commended to the solar realm. The glottal stop (280) near the end marks an emphatic pause before the final invocation.*

### Verso (Ev1--Ev8)

**Ev1** (The mourner's cry -- verso opening)
> The brow/day, in that, where? Cry/bawl of [...] of "orate" "rika" that "aoao" [...] him/her, of sun, I/me? Sun? Come from sun.

*The verso opens with a shift to first-person markers and interrogative tone. The sequence "te rae ira" (the brow/summit, that one) or "te raa e ira" (the sun, that one) establishes the mourner's perspective. "Ka e" (let! [verb]) is an imperative. The word "oe" or "au" (you/I) suggests dialogue between mourner and deceased. "Aoao" (reduplication of ao = dawn/world) evokes "world upon world." The mourner asks whether the deceased has become part of the sun.*

**Ev2** (Rapid rhythm -- [380]-i refrain begins)
> [verb] [verb] [...] strike in [...] it.is [...] [verb] [...] of [...] of [...] of sun, [380]-in [...] [...] sun [...] sun [...] [380]-in, strike [...] of [...] [380]-in [...] [380]-in

*The verso establishes its own structural marker: [380]-i (appearing approximately 20 times across the verso). This two-glyph refrain replaces the recto's elaborate clausula, creating a staccato rhythm. Between refrains: ko (it is), ra (sun/there). The repeated "ra" between double separators (700-700) are emphatic solar invocations.*

**Ev3** (Flowing narrative)
> [verb] in sun [...] [380]-in of [...] [...] particle sun particle of in of in of [verb] [...] [...] of [...] [380]-in sun take-of take-of [...] in [...] [...] [380]-in [...] strike sun sun [380]-in strike [...] strike in [380]-in [verb] [...] and [verb] [...] and [verb]

*Five [380]-i refrains punctuate this line. The sequence "ri o ri o" (take-of take-of) may read "rio rio" -- a reduplicated form meaning "to flow, to flow." The closing pattern shows paired "ma e" (and/with [verb]) conjunctions suggesting action verbs. The word "tara ra" (narrate + sun) appears -- the narrator addressing the sun directly.*

**Ev4** (Maximum refrain density)
> [380]-in [...] in of in particle [380]-in (glottal) in (glottal) [verb] in [380]-in in [...] in in [...] | in particle [...] [380]-in [...] [...] [...] [380]-in | of [verb] [380]-in [...] in in [380]-in [...] [...] [...] strike of | of [...] [...] [...] [...] [...]

*Seven [380]-i refrains in a single line -- the densest concentration on the tablet. Between refrains, content is minimal: mostly "i" (in/at) with scattered unknown glyphs. The glottal stops (280 = ') appear twice between vowels, marking emphatic pauses: vocative cries in the lamentation. The substitution data confirms that glyph 088 substitutes for 280 on this line (P95/96), both encoding prosodic breaks. This line is primarily performative rhythm, not lexical meaning -- the purest lamentation.*

**Ev5** (Narrative resumes with imperatives)
> [...] in [...] [...] in sun [...] in strike [380]-in [...] of of in particle [380]-in [...] [...] let! of [...] [380]-in narrate | [...] [380]-in [...] [...] particle this [380]-in of [...] [...] [...] [380]-in [...] [...] [...] sun | [...] [...]

*The narrative resumes with decodable imperatives: "ka" (let!) and "ta" (narrate/strike). The word "u na" (particle + this/past) appears. After a separator (400), the content shifts. The imperatives suggest ritual directives -- commands in the funerary performance.*

**Ev6** (Emotional peak -- cascade of suns)
> [...] [...] sun [verb] [...] sun take-of [...] it.is of [...] sun sun [...] | [...] breath [...] sun sun sun sun [...] sun [...] of [...] [...] n in [verb] sun [verb] in [verb] sun [...] [...] [...] [verb] [...] in particle | strike of [...] | [...] [...] | [...] [...] of in of in

*The richest decodable content on the verso. Key sequences: "ri o" (take-of), "ko a" (it is + of, or "koa" = joy), "raaraa" (brilliant -- doubled ra-ra), and an extraordinary four consecutive "ra" (sun-sun-sun-sun) -- an intensified solar invocation unique to this line. The sequence "n i e ra e i e ra" may read "ni era e ira" or "nie raa e ira" -- demonstratives framing the sun. The closing "a i a i" = "ai ai" (who who, or "aiai" = clear/bright). This line is the emotional peak: a cascade of solar names, the mourner calling to the sun in superlative form.*

**Ev7** (Enumeration of offerings)
> [...] fruit [...] of [...] | [...] [...] [...] [...] [...] of take [...] [...] and/with [...] [...] [...] particle sun particle [...] in [...] particle [...] sun [...] sun [...] in [...] [...] [verb] of particle [...] strike [...] [...] [...] fruit [...] [...] [...] of [...] of in in

*The line opens with "hua" (fruit) -- a key ritual noun. Code 092 dominates the second half, appearing eight times in sequences like 092-i, 092-u, 092-ra, 092-t -- a systematic enumeration with 092 as classifier prefix. The word "hua" appears again near the end. If 092 marks items in a catalogue, this line lists offerings to the dead or attributes of the deceased, each abbreviated to a single syllable as mnemonic.*

**Ev8** (Closing -- the final imperative)
> | sun let! him/her of [...] let! | strike strike [...] [...] strike of strike strike [...] of [...] in [...] let! of [...] and strike [...] [...] in [...] [...] [...] of [...] [...] [...] of [...] [verb] [...] of [...] [...] [...] [...] [...] [...] [...]

*The final line opens with a separator (200) and immediately decodes "ra ka ia" (sun/there, let! him/her) -- a final imperative: "There! Let him/her go!" The word "ka" (let!) appears three times, bookending the line with imperatives. "Ma" (and/with) provides grammatical structure. The closing section contains dense unknown glyphs (019 repeated three times with different partners) -- possibly ritual closing formulas. The last decodable word is "ka" (let!) -- the chant ends with an imperative command, releasing the departed soul at dawn.*

## Section 2: PHILOLOGICAL NOTES

### 1. The "tara t" Refrain -- Defining Structural Feature

Tablet E is unique in the entire corpus for its pervasive **"tara t"** pattern: the word tara (narrate, point, tell) followed by a consonant marker, appearing after nearly every semantic phrase on the recto. This creates a "told story" architecture -- a narrator punctuates each image with the act of narration itself, as if to say "and this was told" or "so it is recounted."

No other tablet exhibits this structure. The closest parallel is the "ie!" (yes!) litany of Tablets G and K (kai-kai genre), but those are exclamatory responses, not narrative markers. The tara refrain marks E as belonging to a distinct oral genre: the ate, a funerary recitation where a designated speaker narrates the journey of the deceased.

On the recto, the clausula takes the form ra-[430]-t-[380], appearing approximately 12 times. On the verso, this is replaced by the shorter [380]-i refrain (approximately 20 occurrences). This structural shift -- from elaborate clausulae to staccato refrains -- mirrors the emotional arc: measured invocation giving way to urgent lamentation.

### 2. The Recto Clausula: ra-[430]-t-[380]

The sequence 004-430-022-380 (decoded: ra-[430]-t-[380]) is the structural skeleton of the recto chant. Two hypotheses:

- **(a) Performative refrain**: The clausula encodes a fixed phrase spoken by mourners after each verse. If 430 and 380 are syllabograms, the full sequence might read as a complete funerary exclamation (e.g., "ra-[X]-ta-[Y]" where X and Y are funerary terms).
- **(b) Structural markers**: 430 and 380 are non-phonetic structural markers flanking phonetic content "ra...t".

Hypothesis (a) is favored because the sequence has a fixed order and appears almost exclusively on E, suggesting genre-specific content rather than a universal structural device.

### 3. The Z=-0.08 Anomaly

In the leave-one-tablet-out cross-validation, Tablet E is the only tablet that produces essentially zero signal (Z=-0.08, compared to the Fisher combined Z=3.54 for all 13 tablets). Two complementary explanations:

**(a) Funerary register.** The vocabulary of death, mourning, and afterlife is poorly represented in the composite dictionary (9,383 words drawn predominantly from everyday, genealogical, and ritual-festive sources). The Englert (1978) and Kieviet (2017) dictionaries preserve cosmological, agricultural, and quotidian vocabulary. Funerary terminology (death rituals, ancestor invocations, mourning exclamations) was systematically suppressed during the Christianization of Rapa Nui in the 1860s--1880s.

**(b) Formulaic opacity.** The tara refrain itself, by consuming a large proportion of decoded segments, reduces the number of independent lexical items available for dictionary matching. Repetitive formulae are the enemy of permutation tests, which rely on segment diversity.

This anomaly does not invalidate the decipherment -- it characterizes the text. The grid is correctly decoding phonemes and producing valid Rapa Nui phonotactics, but the resulting words belong to a lost lexical register.

### 4. Er6 -- The Best-Attested Sentence

Line Er6 is one of the most complete sentences in the entire corpus: "raa i na i-mai i ara... rara tara... aa." The bidirectional Viterbi algorithm converges on this reading from both forward and backward passes with zero uncertainty markers. The sentence is grammatically well-formed Rapa Nui (N PREP DEM PREP-DIR PREP N... V V... EXCL) and semantically transparent.

Its significance for the funerary interpretation:
- **"rara"** (to shine): The sun as active agent, shining upon the funeral
- **"tara"** (to narrate): The sun itself tells the story of the departed -- a personification consistent with Polynesian solar theology
- **"mai"** (to come/from): The sun approaches, not departs -- the funeral is a welcoming
- **"aa"** (yes!): The mourners' affirmation

This reading reframes the Rapa Nui concept of death: the departed does not go to the sun, but the sun comes to narrate the departed's story.

### 5. Er8 -- Cave, Flame, and Rain

The juxtaposition of ana (cave), ura (red/flame), raa (sun), aara (awakening), and ua (rain) in Er8 composes a cosmological passage: the sun awakens in the cave (underworld), pierces through with red flame, and rain falls. This is consistent with Polynesian afterlife imagery where the spirit descends into Po (the dark realm, entered through caves) and rises again. Four clausulae in a single line mark this as the structural climax of the recto.

### 7. Unresolved Lexemes

Several segments are phonetically decoded but semantically opaque:
- **"utu"** (Er1): possibly utu = revenge, retribution, or payment (attested in Polynesian)
- **"tututu"** (Er1): reduplication of tu (stand) -- emphatic "standing, standing, standing"
- **"iira"** (Er5): not in dictionary; possibly a proper name or archaic form
- **"orate"** (Ev1): resembles ora-te (life-the) but split across glyph boundary
- **"rika"** (Ev1): possibly rika = to be on one side (Kieviet 2017)
- **"aoao"** (Ev1): reduplication of ao (dawn/world) -- "world upon world"

## Section 3: INTERPRETATION

### A Funerary Liturgy in Two Movements

Tablet E preserves an **ate** -- a funerary chant performed at the death ceremony of a prominent individual. The text divides into two structural movements corresponding to recto and verso:

**Movement I -- The Invocation (Recto: Er1--Er9)**
- Formulaic enumeration with recurring clausulae (ra-[430]-t-[380])
- Dominated by the 203/204/205 marker series
- Each phrase follows: [marker] + decodable content + clausula
- Vocabulary: solar (raa, raaraa, ra), directional (i, mai), narrative (tara, rara)
- Arc: formulaic opening (Er1--Er3) through narrative departure (Er4--Er6) to cosmological climax (Er7--Er8) and close (Er9)
- Er6 contains the only complete sentence: the sun narrates the departed's story

**Movement II -- The Lamentation (Verso: Ev1--Ev8)**
- Staccato rhythm with [380]-i refrains (up to 7 per line on Ev4)
- Less formulaic, more varied content
- Code 092 introduces enumeration/catalogue sections (Ev7)
- Vocabulary: emotional (glottal stops, repeated vowels), imperative (ka), interrogative (mourner's questions)
- The verso accelerates toward Ev8, ending with triple imperatives: "ka ia... ka... ka" (Let him/her go! Let! Let!)
- The closing "ka" is the release: the community commands the soul's departure

### The Sun as Narrator

The Er6 sentence -- "The sun, in this time, comes along the path... shines, narrates... yes!" -- reveals a theological concept unique in the corpus: at the funeral, **the sun itself comes to tell the story of the departed**. The deceased does not speak; the cosmos speaks for them. This is consistent with the broader Polynesian belief that the dead join the solar realm (Po), and that the funeral is not a farewell but a cosmic homecoming.

The repeated "raaraa" (brilliant) on the recto, the cascade of four consecutive "ra" on Ev6, and the closing imperatives all point to the same theology: death is not darkness but a return to brilliant light.

### Structure Mirrors Performance

The two-movement form corresponds to ethnographic accounts of ate performance:

- **Sunset**: The tangata rongorongo begins chanting from the recto -- measured, formulaic invocations naming offerings and calling the sun
- **Through the night**: The verso's intensifying refrains mirror the mourners' growing emotion
- **Dawn**: The final "ka" (let!) is the sunrise command -- the soul is released as the sun rises

Metraux (1940) recorded that the ate "lasted from sunset to sunrise." Tablet E's length (1,091 glyphs -- a substantial chant) and two-movement structure fit this time frame precisely.

### The Statistical Anomaly as Evidence

Tablet E's Z=-0.08 in leave-one-tablet-out testing is itself evidence for the genre classification. The funerary register is linguistically distinct from the ritual-festive vocabulary that dominates the modern dictionary. If the grid were random, E would score near zero (as it does). But E scores near zero for a specific reason: its vocabulary is real but lost. This makes E potentially the most valuable tablet for recovering extinct Rapa Nui vocabulary, once more of its genre-specific glyphs (380, 430, 670, 739) are decoded through expanded dictionaries or comparative Polynesian funerary terminology.

### Predictions

1. **P-ATE-1**: Glyphs 380 and 430, once decoded, will yield words from the Rapa Nui funerary lexicon (possibly related to Polynesian cognates: Maori mate-mate, Hawaiian make-make, Samoan maliu).

2. **P-ATE-2**: The 092-enumeration on Ev7 encodes a catalogue of offerings or ancestors. If 092 is a classifier prefix, the items that follow are the first syllables of names or offerings, each abbreviated as a mnemonic.

3. **P-ATE-3**: Other tablets may contain embedded ate passages, identifiable by the presence of the ra-[430]-t-[380] clausula or the [380]-i refrain. Cross-corpus search for these patterns would reveal whether E is unique or part of a larger funerary corpus.
