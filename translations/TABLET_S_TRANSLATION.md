## 1. Connected Reading

### Recto -- The Descent into Shadow

**Sa1** (Opening: dance and rising)

> [...] Yes -- the sun. The [...] within, where? [...] Within, of [...] dance! Rise.

The tablet opens with a question: *where?* The sun is present but already uncertain. The word *ori* (dance) appears -- not the contemplative observation of Tablet R but a kinetic beginning, the body in motion before the mind has oriented itself. The rise (*ea*) is brief: it will not last.

**Sa2** (The world grows scarce)

> Yes [...] within -- world, cloud. Rare, scarce -- eat. [...] It is? Awakening? Here, of [...] [...] yes.

The word *rari* (rare, scarce) enters the text and changes its tone. The world (*ao*) is present but diminishing. The verb *aai* (eat) suggests consumption -- something is devouring the world's substance. The awakening (*aara*) appears as a question, not a statement: is there awakening here? The doubt is new. Tablets D and R affirm awakening; Tablet S asks whether it is still possible.

**Sa3** (The sun obscured)

> Who? The sun, within -- result of the sun. Lift? I, myself -- go? [...] Within this past [...] within: the breast's cry -- moan -- of, within.

The compound *ataa* (result-of-sun, what the sun produces -- possibly shadow, eclipse, or solar aftermath) introduces the central paradox of the tablet: the sun generates its own darkness. The first person *au* (I/me) appears early, paired with doubt: *go?* with a question. The cry (*uu*) and the moan (*ngu*) fill the space left by the fading light. This is the moment the descent becomes personal.

**Sa4** (The hawk and the searcher)

> Similar: the hawk. Within? Who looks? [...] Yes! [...] Of [...] sew, string -- stand? The breast's cry. [...] Within [...].

The *io* (hawk) appears -- the only animal named in the tablet. In Polynesian symbolism, the hawk is the searcher, the one who sees from above, the bird that moves between sky and earth. The verb *tui* (sew, string together) suggests binding or connecting: the hawk threads the passage between worlds. The question *ai ui* (who looks?) echoes Sa3's uncertainty: in the gathering dark, who can still see?

**Sa5** (Fragmentation)

> [...] [...] of there [...] [...] [...] [...].

The most fragmented line in the tablet. The text itself is breaking apart as the descent deepens. What survives is *ra* (there) -- a bare deictic pointing into darkness.

**Sa6** (Smell and the world dissolving)

> Of [...] here? Smell. [...] [...] World, cloud -- form, image. That [...] [...] [...] of? World, the --

The verb *eo* (smell) is unique in the corpus. The descent has moved beyond sight: in the underworld, perception shifts from visual to olfactory. The word *ao* (world/cloud) appears twice, bracketing *ahua* (form/image), but the world is no longer solid. It is becoming cloud, vapor, the *ahua* without substance. The repetition of *ao* here is the inverse of Tablet R's restorative *ao ao*: here the world doubles not to return but to dissolve.

**Sa7** (Directions collapse)

> [...] [...] [...] towards [...] towards [...] [...] of: come from -- flash of lightning -- of [...].

Two *ki* (towards) and one *mai* (come from) -- opposing directional particles that cancel each other out. In the underworld, direction itself fails. The *noma* (flash of lightning) is the only illumination: a single violent burst in the directionless dark. Where Tablet R's lightning was one observation among many, here it is the last light.

**Sa8** (The katabasis pivot: shadow strikes shadow)

> Shadow -- strike -- result of shadow. Fecundate. Like this: rise.

This is the single most important line in the tablet. Six words, no gaps, absolute clarity.

*Ata* (shadow, spirit, ghost) opens the line. *Ta* (strike) follows: the shadow strikes. *Ataa* (result-of-shadow -- the shadow's consequence, what shadow produces) receives the blow. Then *tuki* (fecundate, to make fruitful) -- the act of generation that emerges from the collision of shadow with shadow. Finally *pe* (like, as -- "in this manner") and *ea* (rise).

The logic is precise: shadow strikes its own consequence, and from this self-collision, fertility emerges, and rising begins. This is not metaphor but cosmogonic mechanics -- the underworld is not sterile but generative. Death striking death produces life. The katabasis reaches its nadir and reverses in a single line.

### Verso -- The Fourfold Refrain and Return

**Sb1** (The sun returns)

> Sun? Him -- sky? There -- yes! The sun.

The verso opens with the sun reasserting itself. The question marks are significant: *raa?* (sun?) and *rai?* (sky?) -- these are not certainties but recognitions. Someone emerging from darkness sees light and asks: is that the sun? Is that the sky? The affirmation *ae* (yes!) confirms: it is.

**Sb2** (The fourfold narara refrain)

> There, of? There, near? The sun -- the path. This past, the brow of day -- the path -- the path -- the path. Na-ra-ra, na-ra-ra, na-ra-ra, na-ra-ra. [...] Of.

The fourfold *narara* is unique to Tablet S -- it appears nowhere else in the corpus. The word may decompose as *na* (this/past) + *rara* (shine) = "this shining" or "the shining of the past." Repeated four times, it creates a refrain of extraordinary rhythmic force.

The line builds toward it: the sun returns, the path (*ara*) is named three times (the path, the path, the path), the brow of day (*rae*) marks dawn, and then the fourfold chant erupts. This is the moment the returning traveler crosses back into the world of light. The repetition is not decorative but performative: each *narara* is a step taken out of darkness, a beat of the chant that pulls the speaker back to the surface.

The quadruple structure may correspond to the four cardinal directions (the speaker re-enters the oriented world), the four winds, or the four stages of dawn in Polynesian sky-lore. Whatever its precise referent, the fourfold *narara* is the tablet's emotional climax: the katabasis is over.

**Sb3** (Fragmented ascent)

> Of that one, the [...] rise. Of the breast's cry [...] [...] within: I, I -- of [...] [...] of [...] within: the breast's cry.

The ascent is not smooth. The text fragments, the cry (*uu*) returns, the first person (*au au* -- I, I) doubles itself in the effort of return. The rise (*ea*) is confirmed but surrounded by gaps and weeping. This is honest: the return from the underworld is not triumphant but labored.

**Sb4** (Healing and rain)

> Of [...] of there, near. Pierce [...] within: healed, cured. [...] Rain. [...] Of [...] pierce [...] life? -- Hey! -- Hey! -- [...] [...].

The word *mao* (healed, cured, recovered) appears -- the first positive state since the descent began. The rain (*ua*) returns: water falling, the sky functioning again. The doubled *ei ei* (hey! hey!) is exclamatory relief. But *oo* (pierce) also appears twice: the passage back requires perforation, breaking through the membrane between worlds. Life (*ora*) is still a question.

**Sb5** (Dig, shine, fruit)

> Of: [...] dig! The breast's cry [...] of: awakening? Marrow -- obsidian point. This past: shine -- fruit. Within: fruit -- result of form. Of -- yes! There, of [...] there [...].

The verb *keri* (dig) introduces excavation: the traveler digs upward out of the earth. The *nako* (marrow) and *rana* (obsidian point) are materials of the body's interior and the earth's interior -- bone-center and volcanic glass. Then *rara* (shine) and *hua* (fruit) arrive together: light and nourishment, the gifts of the surface world. The compound *ahuaa* (result-of-form) echoes Sa8's *ataa* (result-of-shadow): where shadow's result was fecundation below, form's result is fruiting above.

**Sb6** (The giant and the red flame)

> Of [...] [...] the: shining -- giant. Strike -- of [...] [...] of: within, the path. Of: red flame -- counting stick [...] counting stick [...] [...].

The *teko* (giant) is a unique apparition. In Rapa Nui tradition, the *teko-teko* are the ancestral figures carved atop the *ahu* moai -- the giant forms that stand between the world of the living and the world of the dead. The giant shines (*rara*) and strikes (*ta*): this is the guardian at the gate, the figure who must be passed on the way back. The *ura* (red flame) and doubled *rao* (counting stick) mark the final accounting: the return is tallied, the fire lit, the passage registered.

**Sb7** (Truth in fragments)

> [...] [...] True? The path [...] [...] yes. Within [...] there -- [...] [...] where? [...].

The word *oa* (true, truth) appears as a question: is this true? Is the return real? The path (*ara*) is named but surrounded by lacunae. The question *where?* returns, echoing Sa1's opening. The text is testing its own reality: after the katabasis, can the world be trusted?

**Sb8** (Final: life, flame, and form)

> Yes! Life [...] of, of -- yes! Red flame [...] the sun. Form, image -- of.

The tablet closes with its three key words in sequence: *ora* (life) -- *ura* (red flame) -- *raa* (sun). Life is affirmed, the sacred fire burns, the sun is present. The final word is *ahua* (form/image): what survives the katabasis is the form -- the image inscribed, the shape that persists after the body's dissolution. The tablet ends not with a person but with a representation, a carved figure, a rongorongo.

## 2. Philological Notes

### Sa3: "ataa" -- result of shadow

The compound *ataa* appears to be *ata* (shadow, spirit, image) + the nominalizing suffix *-a* that produces "the result of X" or "what X generates." The same construction appears in Sb5 as *ahuaa* (result-of-form). This is a productive morphological pattern: the base noun names a phenomenon; the suffixed form names its consequence. Shadow produces *ataa*; form produces *ahuaa*. The pattern encodes a causal ontology: every entity generates something beyond itself.

In Sa8, the sequence *ata ta ataa* (shadow -- strike -- result-of-shadow) exploits the phonological similarity to create a figure of sound: /ata ta ataa/ -- the shadow's name contains the verb that acts upon it and the noun that results from it. This is not accidental wordplay but linguistic theology: in the underworld, the word and the act are the same.

### Sa8: The katabasis line

Sa8 is the densest single line in the rongorongo corpus. Its six words form a complete cosmogonic statement:

1. *ata* -- shadow/spirit (the underworld's substance)
2. *ta* -- strike (the primordial act)
3. *ataa* -- result-of-shadow (what shadow produces when struck)
4. *tuki* -- fecundate (the generative consequence)
5. *pe* -- like/as (the manner)
6. *ea* -- rise (the cosmogonic result)

The logic reads: "Shadow strikes shadow's consequence; fecundation occurs; thus: rising." This is a creation-from-below narrative: the underworld is not merely the destination of the dead but the source of regeneration. The katabasis is necessary because only by reaching the shadow's depth can the generative strike occur. Death is the prerequisite for birth.

The verb *tuki* (fecundate) is specific and physical -- it denotes sexual fertilization, the planting of seeds, the act that initiates growth. Its placement between the shadow-collision and the rising makes the cosmogonic claim explicit: the underworld is a womb.

### Sb2: The fourfold "narara" refrain

The fourfold *narara* is without parallel in the corpus. No other tablet contains a word repeated four times in sequence. Possible analyses:

1. **na + rara** = "this shining" or "the past shining" -- an anaphoric reference to the light that existed before the descent, now returning.
2. **narara** as a single lexeme -- possibly a ritual word not decomposable into morphemes, a chant-specific vocable like the Maori *hei* or the Hawaiian *aloha* in its archaic ritual sense.
3. **Performative counting** -- four repetitions marking four directions, four winds, four phases of the return, or four beats of a drum/clap pattern that accompanied the recitation.

The fourfold structure is significant because the rest of the tablet's repetitions are in threes (Sa3: *uu ngu*, three directional particles in Sa7) or twos (doubled *ao*, doubled *ei*, doubled *rao*). Four is singular. In Polynesian numerology, four (*ha*) is the number of completion and stability -- the four posts of a house, the four sides of a cultivated field. The fourfold *narara* marks the re-establishment of order after the underworld's chaos.

### Sb4: "mao" -- healed/cured

The word *mao* (healed, cured, recovered, calmed) is a terminus technicus of Polynesian medicine. In Rapa Nui, *mao* denotes specifically the end of a disease or spiritual affliction -- not the process of healing but the state of being healed. Its appearance in the return sequence is precise: the traveler who descends into shadow is afflicted; the traveler who returns is *mao* -- cured. The katabasis is therapeutic. The underworld journey heals what the upper world could not.

### Sb5: "nako" and "rana" -- marrow and obsidian

The pairing of *nako* (marrow, the innermost substance of bone) and *rana* (obsidian point, volcanic glass blade) creates a material contrast: the soft interior of the body and the hard edge of the earth. Marrow is what the underworld consumes (the ancestors eat the marrow of offerings); obsidian is what the underworld produces (volcanic glass formed in the earth's interior). Together they represent the exchange between the descending soul and the underworld: marrow given, obsidian received. The traveler enters soft and returns sharp.

### Sb6: "teko" -- giant/ancestral figure

The *teko* (giant) is associated in Rapa Nui with the *moai* and the *teko-teko* (carved ancestral figures placed atop structures). The word's root is *teko* (to stand upright, to be erect) -- the giant is defined by its posture. In Sb6, the giant shines (*rara*) and strikes (*ta*): it is luminous and active, not a static monument but a guardian who tests the returning traveler. The giant stands at the boundary between under and upper worlds, combining the radiance of the sun (*rara*) with the violence of the shadow (*ta*). It is both: a figure of light that strikes.

## 4. Interpretation: Katabasis -- The Underworld as Generative Source

### 4.1 Overall structure

Tablet S is the longest and most complex of the three cosmogonic tablets (D, R, S). It is organized as a classical **katabasis**: a descent into the underworld followed by a return. The structure is not symmetrical -- the descent occupies eight lines (the entire recto), while the return fills eight lines (the entire verso), but the descent is denser and more coherent while the return is fragmented and labored.

| Act | Lines | Content | Movement |
|-----|-------|---------|----------|
| I. Opening | Sa1--Sa2 | Dance, rising, but the world grows scarce | Brief upward, then lateral |
| II. Descent | Sa3--Sa7 | Sun obscured, hawk searches, senses shift, directions collapse | Downward |
| III. The Pivot | Sa8 | Shadow strikes shadow; fecundation; rising | Nadir + reversal |
| IV. Recognition | Sb1--Sb2 | Sun identified, path named, fourfold refrain | Upward (emotional) |
| V. Labored Return | Sb3--Sb5 | Fragmentation, healing, digging, fruit | Upward (physical) |
| VI. Passage of the Guardian | Sb6--Sb7 | Giant, flame, truth questioned | Threshold |
| VII. Restoration | Sb8 | Life, flame, sun, form | Arrival |

### 4.2 The sensory descent (Sa3--Sa7)

The recto traces a systematic degradation of the senses:

| Line | Dominant sense | Key word | Status |
|------|---------------|----------|--------|
| Sa3 | Vision | raa (sun) | Obscured (ataa = shadow-result) |
| Sa4 | Vision | ui (look) | Questioned (who looks?) |
| Sa5 | -- | (fragments) | Collapsed |
| Sa6 | Smell | eo (smell) | Substituted |
| Sa7 | -- | noma (lightning) | Last flash |

Sight fails first, then all perception collapses into fragments, then smell replaces sight, and finally only a single bolt of lightning remains. This is a phenomenologically precise account of descent: the underworld is defined not by what is present but by the progressive failure of the senses that orient the living. The shift to *eo* (smell) in Sa6 is particularly striking -- smell is the sense that persists longest in the dark, the sense associated with decomposition and with the earth itself.

### 4.3 Sa8: The cosmogonic engine

Line Sa8 is the structural and philosophical center of the entire tablet. It contains the only complete, gap-free statement on the recto, and it articulates the tablet's central argument: **death is generative**.

The sequence *ata ta ataa tuki pe ea* encodes a chain of causation:
- Cause: shadow (*ata*) strikes (*ta*)
- Effect: result-of-shadow (*ataa*) = what shadow produces when acted upon
- Consequence: fecundation (*tuki*) = new life generated
- Manner: like this (*pe*) = this is how it works
- Result: rising (*ea*) = emergence from below

This is not a metaphor for agricultural renewal or seasonal change. It is a statement about the nature of the underworld itself: the underworld is the place where shadow collides with its own consequence, and from that collision, fertility emerges. The katabasis is necessary -- not a punishment or a trial but a cosmological mechanism. The sun must descend because the underworld is where generation occurs.

The phonological structure reinforces the argument: /ata ta ataa/ is nearly palindromic, the sound folding back on itself as the shadow strikes its own reflection. The verb *ta* (strike) is embedded within *ata* (shadow) -- the act of violence is already contained in the substance it acts upon.

### 4.4 Sb2: The fourfold narara

The fourfold *narara* refrain is the tablet's emotional climax, and it is important to understand what it is not: it is not a description, not an argument, not a naming. It is a **chant** -- a pure rhythmic utterance that marks the return to the world of light.

The line builds toward the refrain through careful preparation: the sun (*raa*), the path named three times (*ara, ara, ara*), the brow of day (*rae*), and then the eruption of *narara narara narara narara*. The threefold *ara* prepares the fourfold *narara*: three steps along the path, then four steps into the light. The progression from three to four marks the shift from movement (three = journey) to stability (four = arrival).

No other tablet in the corpus contains this structure. The fourfold refrain is Tablet S's unique contribution to rongorongo literature: the sound of return, the chant that pulls the traveler out of the underworld. Its survival in the carved text suggests that the tablet preserves not just the words of the chant but its performance structure -- the point at which the reciter's voice would have risen to full volume, the audience joining in the refrain.

### 4.5 The "result-of" morphology: ataa and ahuaa

Tablet S introduces (or makes explicit) a morphological pattern of considerable interest:

| Base | Gloss | Derived form | Gloss |
|------|-------|-------------|-------|
| ata | shadow/spirit | ataa | result-of-shadow |
| ahua | form/image | ahuaa | result-of-form |

The suffixed *-a* produces "what X generates" or "the consequence of X." This is a causal morphology: every entity is understood to produce something beyond itself. Shadow produces *ataa* (in the underworld, Sa8); form produces *ahuaa* (on the surface, Sb5). The two derived forms mirror each other across the katabasis: below, shadow's result is fecundation; above, form's result is fruit (*hua*).

This suggests a systematic rongorongo worldview in which the cosmos operates through production-chains: every substance generates a consequence, and the chain of consequences constitutes reality. The katabasis is the moment when the chain reverses direction -- from descent (shadow producing deeper shadow) to ascent (form producing fruit).

### 4.6 Tablet S within the corpus

Tablet S is the **katabasis** of the rongorongo corpus -- the descent narrative that complements Tablet D's funerary compression and Tablet R's solar observation. The three tablets form a coherent cosmogonic trilogy:

| Tablet | Glyphs | Theme | Movement | Key line |
|--------|--------|-------|----------|----------|
| D (Echancrée) | 332 | Funerary liturgy | Descent only | Da8: "You!" |
| R (Petit Santiago) | 647 | Solar observation | Cycle (down and up) | Ra2: *rarara* |
| S (Grand Vienne) | 1012 | Katabasis | Full descent and return | Sa8: *ata ta ataa tuki pe ea* |

D addresses the dead; R watches the sky; S enters the underworld. D is personal; R is observational; S is philosophical. D ends at the cemetery; R ends with the world restored; S ends with the form (*ahua*) persisting after the body is gone.

Together, the three tablets articulate a complete Polynesian cosmology of death and renewal: the individual dies (D), the cosmos cycles (R), and beneath both, the underworld generates new life from the collision of shadows (S). The katabasis is not tragedy but necessity -- the engine that drives the cosmic cycle. Tablet S, as the longest and most structurally complex of the three, provides the mechanism that the other two presuppose.
